# Gap Trading Strategies for Swing Trading

Prepared by Codex on 2026-03-10.

Price gaps — discontinuities between one session's close and the next session's open — create both significant risk and unique opportunity for swing traders. This file covers gap types, trading strategies, and risk management specific to overnight, weekend, and earnings gaps.

---

## 1. What Are Price Gaps?

**Definition:** A gap occurs when an asset opens at a price significantly different from its previous close, creating a visible "gap" on the chart with no trading activity in between.

**Visual representation:**
```
Day 1 Close: $50
Day 2 Open:  $53  ← Gap up of $3
```

**Why gaps matter for swing traders:**
- Overnight and weekend holding is unavoidable in swing trading
- Gaps can invalidate stop-losses (stop at $48, gap down open at $45)
- Gaps create directional momentum that can be traded
- Gap behavior (fill vs. continuation) provides tradable patterns

---

## 2. Types of Gaps

### Common Gap (Area Gap)

**Characteristics:**
- Small gap (typically <2%) during normal trading
- Occurs within consolidation or range
- No significant news or catalyst
- Usually fills quickly (within hours or days)

**Trading implication:** Low significance, often noise. Not a primary swing trade signal.

---

### Breakaway Gap

**Characteristics:**
- Occurs at the end of a consolidation or base
- Accompanied by high volume
- Clear catalyst (earnings, news, sector strength)
- Typically does NOT fill (or fills much later)
- Signals start of a new trend

**Trading implication:** **Strong bullish or bearish signal.** These are the gaps swing traders want to trade with, not against.

**Example:**
- Stock consolidates $48-$52 for 8 weeks
- Earnings beat → Gaps to $56 on 3x normal volume
- Consolidates $55-$57 for 2-3 days
- **Trade:** Enter long on breakout above consolidation, stop below gap

---

### Runaway Gap (Continuation Gap)

**Characteristics:**
- Occurs in middle of existing trend
- Confirms trend strength
- Often preceded and followed by strong directional moves
- Higher volume than common gaps
- May partially fill but trend resumes

**Trading implication:** **Trend confirmation.** Opportunity to add to existing position or enter new position in direction of trend.

**Example:**
- Stock in uptrend from $40 → $50 over 2 weeks
- Gaps from $50 → $53 on analyst upgrade
- Pulls back to $51, then resumes to $60
- **Trade:** Enter on pullback to gap area, stop below gap fill

---

### Exhaustion Gap

**Characteristics:**
- Occurs at end of extended trend
- Often largest gap of the move
- Volume spikes but trend soon reverses
- Marks climax of buying/selling pressure
- Usually fills within days to weeks

**Trading implication:** **Reversal signal.** Fade the gap, but with tight stops (can extend briefly before reversing).

**Example:**
- Stock rallies from $30 → $65 over 6 weeks (parabolic)
- Gaps from $65 → $72 on "blow-off top" volume
- Next day fails to hold gains, closes at $68
- **Trade:** Short at $68 with stop at $73, target gap fill back to $65

---

## 3. Gap Fill Statistics and Probability

### The "Gap Fill" Concept

**Definition:** A gap is "filled" when price returns to the prior session's close level.

**Common belief:** "Gaps always fill."

**Reality:** Not all gaps fill, and timing varies greatly.

### Research Findings

**Common gaps:** ~70-80% fill within a few days

**Breakaway gaps:** ~30-40% fill within first month (most do NOT fill immediately)

**Runaway gaps:** ~50% partially fill (pullback to gap zone), but trend usually resumes

**Exhaustion gaps:** ~80-90% fill rapidly (within 1-2 weeks)

### Implication for Swing Traders

**Strategy:**
- Trade WITH breakaway and runaway gaps (do not fade them)
- Trade AGAINST exhaustion gaps (fade after confirmation of reversal)
- Ignore common gaps unless part of larger setup

## 4. Overnight Gap Trading Strategies

### Strategy 1: Gap and Go (Continuation)

**Setup:**
- Stock gaps up 2-5% on significant news (earnings, upgrade, sector strength)
- Pre-market volume confirms interest
- Opens strong, holds near the high

**Entry rules:**
- Wait for first 15-30 minutes to establish range
- Enter when price breaks above first 30-minute high
- Volume must be above average

**Stop:** Below the low of first 30 minutes or gap fill level

**Target:** 1.5-2x the initial risk, or prior resistance level

**Best for:** Stocks with clear catalyst and strong pre-market action

---

### Strategy 2: Gap Fill / Mean Reversion

**Setup:**
- Stock gaps up 3-5% with no significant news
- Opens at high of day, then immediate selling
- Volume declining as day progresses

**Entry rules:**
- Enter short when price breaks below prior day's close (gap filled)
- Or enter long when gap fully filled and stock shows reversal candle

**Stop:** Just beyond gap high (for shorts) or below gap fill low (for longs)

**Target:** Mid-point of gap or opposite side of gap

**Best for:** Gaps that appear overextended with no fundamental support

---

### Strategy 3: Partial Gap Fill + Continuation

**Setup:**
- Stock gaps up on news
- Pulls back 30-50% of gap size
- Finds support and resumes upward

**Entry rules:**
- Wait for gap to pull back to 38-50% Fibonacci retracement of gap
- Enter when reversal candle forms (e.g., hammer, bullish engulfing)
- Volume should decline on pullback, increase on resumption

**Stop:** Below gap fill level

**Target:** New high above gap open, or measured move (gap size added to breakout)

**Best for:** High-quality stocks with strong catalysts

## 5. Weekend Gap Trading (Primarily Forex and Crypto)

### Why Weekend Gaps Occur

**Causes:**
- Economic data released over weekend (rare)
- Geopolitical events (elections, conflicts, policy announcements)
- Crypto never closes, but forex markets do (Sunday open can gap)
- Major news announced when markets closed (e.g., Saturday morning)

### Risk Management for Weekend Gaps

**Rule 1: Reduce position size going into weekends**
- If major news or event expected, cut size by 50% or exit entirely
- Unpredictable gaps can blow through stops

**Rule 2: Use wider stops**
- If holding through weekend, use weekly stop levels (not daily)
- Small gaps are routine; position sizing should account for this

**Rule 3: Avoid low-liquidity pairs/assets**
- Stick to major forex pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/USD) if holding through weekend
- Avoid exotic pairs and low-cap cryptos

### Weekend Gap Trading Strategy

**Setup:**
- Market gaps significantly on Sunday night open (forex) or Monday morning (stocks)
- No sustained news flow to support gap

**Execution:**
- **If gap >1.5% with no news:** Fade the gap (mean reversion trade)
- **If gap >2% with major news:** Trade with the gap (continuation)

**Risk:** Gaps with true fundamental drivers often do NOT fill quickly. Ensure catalyst assessment before fading.

## 6. Earnings Gap Trading Strategies

Earnings gaps are the most significant and tradable gaps for swing traders.

### Pre-Earnings Setup (Not Recommended for Most Swing Traders)

**Risk:** Binary event with ~50/50 direction, high IV crush on options

**If trading:**
- Only trade companies with consistent beat history
- Use small position size (0.5% risk)
- Exit day of earnings before close OR use wide stops beyond expected move

### Post-Earnings Gap Trading (Preferred)

#### Strategy 1: Earnings Gap Continuation

**Setup:**
- Stock beats earnings and raises guidance
- Gaps up 8%+ on strong volume
- Consolidates for 1-3 days after gap

**Entry:**
- Wait for 2-3 day consolidation (flag or tight range)
- Enter on breakout above consolidation high
- Volume should confirm breakout

**Stop:** Below consolidation low or gap fill level

**Target:** Measured move (gap size projected from consolidation breakout)

**Duration:** 5-15 days typical

**Example:**
- Stock at $50, earnings → Gaps to $55
- Consolidates $54-$56 for 3 days
- Breaks out at $56.50 → Target $61-$62 (gap size = $5, projected from $56)

---

#### Strategy 2: Earnings Gap Fade

**Setup:**
- Stock gaps up significantly (10%+) but news is mixed or already priced in
- First day post-gap shows long upper wick (rejection)
- Volume declines after initial spike

**Entry:**
- Enter short when price closes below gap day's low
- Or wait for full gap fill and enter short on failed bounce

**Stop:** Above gap day's high

**Target:** 50% gap fill initially, full gap fill as secondary target

**Risk:** Can be painful if gap is justified; use tight stops

## 7. Advanced Gap Concepts

### Gap Size and Behavior

**Small gaps (1-2%):**
- Often fill within same day or week
- Low predictive value
- Noise more than signal

**Medium gaps (3-6%):**
- Require catalyst to sustain
- May partially fill then resume
- Tradable but require confirmation

**Large gaps (7%+):**
- Usually major news or earnings
- Less likely to fill quickly if justified
- Strong directional signal

**Extreme gaps (15%+):**
- Often overextended
- High probability of at least partial fill
- Fade candidates if no ongoing catalyst

---

### Gap Volume Analysis

**High volume gap (2x+ average):**
- Confirms institutional participation
- More likely to sustain direction
- Breakaway or runaway gap candidate

**Low volume gap (<1x average):**
- Retail-driven or thin market
- Higher probability of fill
- Less reliable signal

---

### Gap and Market Context

**Gap with the trend:**
- Much more likely to hold and extend
- Trade with it (continuation)

**Gap against the trend:**
- Often fills as trend reasserts
- Fade opportunity

**Example:**
- Stock in strong downtrend
- Gaps up 4% on minor good news
- Usually fills and downtrend resumes

---

## 8. Multi-Timeframe Gap Analysis

### Higher Timeframe Context

**Weekly chart shows:**
- Is this gap breaking out of multi-week base? (Breakaway)
- Is gap within existing weekly uptrend? (Runaway)
- Is gap after parabolic weekly move? (Exhaustion)

**Daily chart shows:**
- Precise gap size and entry/exit levels
- Consolidation patterns post-gap

**4-Hour or 1-Hour chart:**
- Fine-tune entry timing
- Identify micro support/resistance within gap zone

**Rule:** Only trade gaps aligned with higher timeframe trend.

---

## 9. Gap Risk Management

### Stop-Loss Placement

**For gap continuation trades (long):**
- Stop below gap fill level (prior day's close)
- Or stop below consolidation low if trading post-gap setup

**For gap fade trades (short):**
- Stop above gap high
- Or stop above key resistance within gap

**Gap stop discipline:**
- Gaps are volatile; give room but respect stop
- If stopped out, do not re-enter impulsively

---

### Position Sizing for Gap Trades

**Overnight gap risk:**
- Assume gap can be up to 2-3% beyond your stop
- Size accordingly: If stop is $2 away, assume potential $3-4 gap loss

**Formula:**
```
Max Position Size = (Account Risk $) / (Stop Distance + Expected Gap Slippage)

Example:
- Account risk: $1,000
- Stop distance: $2
- Expected gap slippage: $1.50
- Position size: $1,000 / $3.50 = 286 shares
```

**This is why swing traders often risk less than day traders on paper, but real risk includes gap potential.**

---

### Gap Protection Techniques

**Technique 1: Scale out before events**
- If holding before known catalyst (earnings, Fed, etc.), reduce by 50%

**Technique 2: Use wider stops**
- Set stops at key weekly or monthly levels, not tight daily levels

**Technique 3: Hedge with options**
- Buy protective puts on high-risk holdings before events
- Costs premium but caps downside

**Technique 4: Avoid concentrated positions**
- Never have >3% of portfolio in one stock near earnings

## 10. Common Gap Trading Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Fading Every Gap

**Problem:** Assuming all gaps fill, especially breakaway gaps on strong catalysts

**Solution:** Classify gap type first. Only fade common gaps and exhaustion gaps.

---

### Mistake 2: Chasing Gaps at the Open

**Problem:** Buying at the open of a gap-up without confirmation, getting stuck at the high

**Solution:** Wait 15-30 minutes for range to establish. Enter on breakout of opening range.

---

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Volume

**Problem:** Trading gaps without volume confirmation

**Solution:** High-volume gaps more reliable. Low-volume gaps more likely to reverse.

---

### Mistake 4: No Stop-Loss

**Problem:** "I'll just wait for the gap to fill" → can take weeks or never happen

**Solution:** Always have a stop. If gap doesn't fill, thesis was wrong. Exit and move on.

---

### Mistake 5: Oversizing Gap Trades

**Problem:** Treating gap trades like normal trades, ignoring added volatility

**Solution:** Reduce position size by 25-50% for gap trades due to higher risk.

## 11. Gap Trading by Asset Class

### Stocks

- Most common gap type for swing traders
- Earnings gaps largest and most tradable
- Overnight gaps (2-3%) are routine

**Best strategy:** Earnings gap continuation, breakaway gap continuation

---

### Forex

- Weekend gaps more common (Sunday night open)
- Typically smaller than stock gaps (0.5-1.5%)
- Often fill quickly due to high liquidity

**Best strategy:** Gap fill / mean reversion on non-news gaps

---

### Crypto

- No gaps (24/7 trading) but "session gaps" when volume shifts between regions
- High volatility makes gap-like moves common

**Adaptation:** Look for sudden 5-10% moves in low-volume hours (similar to gap psychology)

---

### Commodities / Futures

- Gaps occur at session open (e.g., Sunday night ES futures)
- Can be extreme (limit up/down moves)
- Often driven by global events (Asia/Europe news impacting US open)

**Best strategy:** Trade with large gaps on macro events, fade small gaps

## 12. Gap Statistics to Know

### Gap Fill Rates (Approximate)

- **Up gaps >3%:** ~50-60% fill within 10 days
- **Down gaps >3%:** ~50-60% fill within 10 days
- **Earnings gaps >7%:** ~40% fill within 30 days
- **Non-news gaps >2%:** ~70-80% fill within 5 days

**Takeaway:** Gaps do often fill, but not always quickly or completely. Context matters more than raw statistics.

---

### Gap and Next-Day Performance

**Research shows:**
- Stocks that gap up >4% on high volume tend to continue upward next day 55-60% of the time (slight edge)
- Stocks that gap up >4% on low volume reverse next day 60-65% of the time

**Implication:** Volume is the key discriminator. High volume = continuation, low volume = reversal.

## 13. Real-World Examples

### Example 1: Breakaway Gap Success (Long)

**Stock:** Tech stock consolidating $80-$85 for 10 weeks  
**Catalyst:** Earnings beat, guidance raise  
**Gap:** Opens at $93 (+9%) on 5x volume  

**Trade:**
- Wait for 3-day consolidation $91-$94
- Enter long at $94.50 on breakout
- Stop at $89 (below gap)
- Target: $105 (measured move)
- Outcome: Reached $107 in 12 days. Exit at $105 = 11% gain, 2.0R

---

### Example 2: Exhaustion Gap Fade (Short)

**Stock:** Stock in parabolic rise $50 → $80 in 4 weeks  
**Gap:** Gaps from $80 → $88 on no new news, just momentum  
**Action:** Opens at $88, spikes to $90, closes at $85 (long upper wick)

**Trade:**
- Enter short at $84 when breaks below previous day's close
- Stop at $91 (above gap high)
- Target: $80 (gap fill)
- Outcome: Filled gap to $80 in 6 days, then continued lower. Exit at $80 = $4 gain, 0.57R

---

### Example 3: Gap Fill Trap (Failed Short)

**Stock:** Gaps up from $60 → $67 on acquisition news  
**Assumption:** "7-point gap, it will fill"  
**Reality:** Acquisition real, stock trends to $75 over next 2 weeks, never fills

**Lesson:** Do not fade gaps with genuine fundamental catalysts. Gap fill is probabilistic, not guaranteed.

## 14. Integration with Swing Trading Strategy

### Gap as Entry Trigger

- Use gap as signal to enter swing trade in direction of trend
- Example: Stock in weekly uptrend, gaps up on earnings → Enter on consolidation breakout

### Gap as Exit Trigger

- If holding position and gap occurs against you, exit immediately
- Example: Long from $50, overnight gap down to $46 on bad news → Exit at open, do not "hope"

### Gap as Regime Indicator

- Cluster of gaps in one direction (e.g., multiple stocks gapping up) signals strong market or sector momentum
- Adjust strategy to momentum-based when gaps cluster

## 16. Bottom Line

Gaps are unavoidable in swing trading and represent both danger (stops invalidated) and opportunity (directional momentum). Success comes from:

1. **Classifying the gap type** (breakaway, runaway, exhaustion, common)
2. **Aligning with trend and context** (don't fade quality breakaway gaps)
3. **Waiting for confirmation** (opening range, consolidation, volume)
4. **Managing risk appropriately** (smaller size, wider stops, gap slippage buffer)
5. **Using gaps as signals within broader strategy** (not standalone trades)

The trader who understands gap psychology — when they signal continuation vs. reversal — gains a significant edge in swing trading.
